How to prepare for land ownership verification

Land ownership verification is the process of confirming that a property legally belongs to a person or organization based on official records and supporting documents. Before starting a verification request, it is important to collect the correct documents and ensure the property details are accurate.

Preparing in advance helps reduce delays, prevents incomplete submissions, and makes the verification process easier for both the applicant and the reviewing authority.

What you should prepare

  • National Identity Card or another valid identification document
  • Copy of the deed or ownership certificate related to the land
  • Survey plan or land parcel reference information, if available
  • Property address, district, or local area details
  • Any previous land-related approvals or supporting legal documents

Before submitting

  • Check that the owner name matches the submitted legal documents
  • Make sure land references and parcel details are clearly written
  • Confirm that uploaded copies are readable and complete
  • Keep additional supporting documents ready in case verification is requested

Preparing for planning approval submissions

Planning approval submissions usually require accurate property details, applicant information, and supporting documents related to the proposed land use or development activity.

Before submission, applicants should make sure all required information is available and organized. This improves the quality of the application and helps reduce delays caused by missing details.

Common preparation steps

  • Identify the property correctly using land or parcel details
  • Prepare site-related drawings, plans, or descriptions if needed
  • Collect owner and applicant identification information
  • Review whether additional permits or supporting approvals may be required
  • Ensure contact details are correct for future updates

Helpful advice

  • Double-check all uploaded documents before submission
  • Use clear file names and readable document scans
  • Keep original versions of documents for later verification
  • Be prepared to provide more documents if requested by the authority
